Kentaro Miura

A Japanese Manga Artist

Here's some description about Kentaro Miura

  • 1966 - Born in Chiba, Chiba Prefecture, Japan.
  • 1976 - At the age of 10, he created his first manga, entitled Miuranger.
  • 1977 - Miura created his second manga, Ken e no Michi ("The Way to the Sword")
  • 1982 - Miura enrolled in an artistic curriculum, where he and his classmates started publishing their works in school booklets
  • 1984 - Miura briefly worked as an assistant to George Morikawa, of Hajime no Ippo fame.
  • 1985 - Miura applied for entrance to the art college of Nihon University.
  • 1988 - While working for Buronson on a project titled King of Wolves ( Ōrō), Miura published a prototype of Berserk in Hakusensha's Monthly ComiComi.
  • 1989 - The full serialization of Berserk, which would become Miura's most famous and successful work, began in Hakusensha's Monthly Animal House
  • 1992 - Miura collaborated with Buronson on the manga Japan, which was also published in Young Animal(New Name of Monthly Animal House).
  • 1997 - Miura supervised the production of a 25-episode anime adaptation of Berserk produced by OLM, Inc
  • 1999 - He also supervised the Dreamcast video game Sword of the Berserk: Guts' Rage.
  • 2002 - Miura received the Award for Excellence at the 6th Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prizes for Berserk.
  • 2006 - Berserk went on frequent and often extended hiatuses, and alternated between monthly and irregular serialization.
  • 2018 - Berserk was collected into 40 tankōbon volumes in Japan, and as of May 2021 it had more than 50 million copies in circulation worldwide, including digital versions
  • 2021 - On May 6, 2021, Miura died due to acute aortic dissection, at the age of 54. His death was publicly announced on May 20, 2021. A private ceremony was held by his family
